MUSTIE Threshold Settings

Customize the age thresholds used to flag items for each subject area

How do these thresholds work? The CREW method recommends different maximum ages for different subject areas. For example, a 10-year-old science book may contain outdated facts, while a 15-year-old history book is often still perfectly fine.

The Max Age column below controls when items in each subject area get flagged as M (Misleading). If an item's age exceeds its subject's max age, it gets the M flag. You can raise or lower any threshold to match your library's weeding policy.

The defaults shown here come from the CREW method guidelines. Click Save Changes to apply your custom values, or Reset to Defaults to go back to the CREW recommendations.

Thresholds by Subject

Max Age controls the M (Misleading) flag. Max Dormant controls how many years with no checkouts before the I flag triggers. Min Checkouts is the minimum checkout count below which an item is considered low-circulation.
