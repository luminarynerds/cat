MUSTIE Weeding

Weeding candidates evaluated using the CREW/MUSTIE framework

What is MUSTIE? MUSTIE is a framework from the CREW method (Continuous Review, Evaluation, and Weeding) used by libraries nationwide. Each letter flags a different reason an item might need to be withdrawn:
  • M — Misleading: Contains outdated or inaccurate information. Flagged automatically for items in fast-changing subjects (Science, Technology, Medicine, Law) that exceed their recommended age limit.
  • U — Ugly: Physically worn or damaged. Approximated here by flagging old items with high circulation (likely handled many times).
  • S — Superseded: A newer edition may exist. Flagged when your catalog has another item with the same title and author but a more recent publication year.
  • T — Trivial: Of no discernible merit. Cannot be detected from catalog data — requires manual review.
  • I — Irrelevant: Doesn't match community needs. Flagged for items with very low or zero checkouts.
  • E — Elsewhere: Readily available from other sources (ILL, digital, etc.). Cannot be detected from catalog data — requires manual review.
The age thresholds vary by subject area based on CREW guidelines. You can customize the thresholds to match your library's policy.
