How to read this report: Each bar represents a broad subject area based on the letters at the start of your call numbers (using the Library of Congress classification system). The longer the bar, the more items you have in that area. A balanced collection doesn't mean every bar is the same size — Literature (P) is usually the largest section for public libraries, for example. What you're looking for are areas that seem surprisingly small or surprisingly large for your community's needs.

The Avg Checkouts column shows how much each subject area is actually being used. A small section with high checkouts may mean patron demand is outpacing your holdings.
