Getting Started

New here? This page walks you through everything step by step.

What is this tool?
This app helps you understand what your library owns, spot gaps where you might need more books, and find items that may be ready to weed — all without connecting to your ILS. You just upload a report file (CSV or Excel) that you've already pulled from your catalog system, and the tool does the rest.

How It Works

1

Pull a report from your ILS

In your catalog system (Sierra, Polaris, Koha, Evergreen, or any other ILS), run a report of all items held at your library. Export it as a CSV or Excel file. Most systems have a "Create Lists" or "Reports" module that can do this.

Tip: The more fields you include, the better the analysis. At minimum, try to include Title, Call Number, and Publication Year. If you can also include Checkouts (circulation count) and Format (material type), you'll unlock even more reports.

2

Upload the file here

Go to the Import Data page and upload your CSV or Excel file. The tool automatically figures out which columns are which — it understands dozens of common column name variations from different ILS systems. You do not need to rename your columns first.

3

Explore the reports

Once your data is loaded, every report in the sidebar lights up. Here's what each one tells you:

  • Dashboard — A quick snapshot: how many items you have, how old they are on average, and how many have never been checked out.
  • Collection Gaps — The main event! Shows you which subject areas are thin, which ones haven't had new material added recently, and which areas are getting old.
  • Subject Balance — A visual bar chart showing how your collection is distributed across broad subject areas (like Science, History, Literature, etc.).
  • Age Distribution — How many items you have from each decade. Helps you see if your collection is skewing too old overall.
  • Format Breakdown — Shows the split between books, DVDs, audiobooks, and other formats, plus which formats get checked out most.
  • Usage Analysis — Your most popular items and circulation patterns by format.
  • Weeding Candidates — Items that are both old and rarely checked out. You can adjust the age and checkout thresholds to match your library's weeding policy.
4

Export your results

Every report page has a Download CSV button so you can save the data to a spreadsheet. This is handy for sharing results with your director, building a purchasing list, or keeping records of weeding decisions. You can also use Ctrl+P (or Cmd+P on Mac) to print any page — the sidebar and buttons will be hidden automatically.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this connect to my ILS?

No. It works entirely with files you've already exported. Your data stays on your computer and is never sent anywhere.

What if my column names are different?

The tool recognizes many common variations (like "Pub Year", "Publication Year", "Year", "Date", etc.). After uploading, check the Column Mapping page to see what was detected. If a field shows as "Empty", it means your file either doesn't have that data or uses a column name the tool didn't recognize — that's OK, the reports just adapt to what's available.

What does "LC classification" mean?

The Library of Congress (LC) classification system assigns letter codes to broad subject areas. For example, "P" = Language & Literature, "Q" = Science, "H" = Social Sciences. The tool reads the letters at the start of your call numbers to figure out what subject area each item belongs to. It also works with Dewey Decimal call numbers by mapping the first digit to the equivalent LC area.

Can I upload a new file to replace the old one?

Yes! Just go to Import Data and upload a new file. It replaces the previous data immediately. This is useful for comparing your collection over time — export a CSV now, export another one in six months, and see how things changed.
